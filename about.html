The 2001 Finals Run

The 2000-2001 season was a magical one for the Philadelphia 76ers, culminating in an NBA Finals appearance.

Led by Allen Iverson, who was named the league's Most Valuable Player, the Sixers finished the regular season with a 56-26 record, the best in the Eastern Conference. Iverson averaged 31.1 points per game, leading the league in scoring.

The team's identity was built around Iverson's scoring prowess and a gritty, defensive-minded supporting cast. The midseason acquisition of Dikembe Mutombo from the Atlanta Hawks bolstered their interior defense and rebounding.

In the playoffs, the Sixers defeated the Indiana Pacers in the first round, the Toronto Raptors in the second round, and the Milwaukee Bucks in the Eastern Conference Finals to advance to the NBA Finals against the Los Angeles Lakers.

Although they lost to the Lakers in five games, the Sixers did manage to win Game 1 in Los Angeles, with Iverson's iconic "step over" of Tyronn Lue becoming one of the most memorable moments in NBA history.

The 2001-2002 Season

Following their Finals appearance, expectations were high for the 2001-2002 Philadelphia 76ers.

However, the season proved challenging. Allen Iverson missed 22 games due to injuries, and the team struggled to find the same chemistry that had propelled them to the Finals the previous year.

Despite the setbacks, the Sixers finished with a respectable 43-39 record, good enough for the 6th seed in the Eastern Conference playoffs.

The team's defensive identity remained intact, as they allowed just 89.4 points per game, among the best in the league. However, their offensive output dipped to 91.4 points per game.

The season ended with a first-round playoff loss to the Boston Celtics in five games, a disappointing conclusion for a team that had reached the NBA Finals just a year earlier.
