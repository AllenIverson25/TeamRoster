Allen Iverson, nicknamed "The Answer," was the heart and soul of the Philadelphia 76ers. The former #1 overall pick in the 1996 NBA Draft led the Sixers to the NBA Finals in 2001 and was named the league's Most Valuable Player that same season.

Career Overview

Allen Ezail Iverson was born on June 7, 1975, in Hampton, Virginia. He was selected by the Philadelphia 76ers with the first overall pick in the 1996 NBA draft. Iverson played 14 seasons in the NBA, where he was an 11-time NBA All-Star and won the All-Star game MVP award in 2001 and 2005.

Iverson was the NBA's Most Valuable Player (MVP) in 2001 when he led the 76ers to the NBA Finals. He was the NBA's scoring champion four times (1999, 2001, 2002, and 2005) and led the league in steals three times (2001, 2002, and 2003).

Known for his speed, scoring ability, and crossover dribble, Iverson was one of the most prolific scorers in NBA history despite his relatively small stature for a professional basketball player (6 feet, 165 pounds). His career scoring average of 26.7 points per game ranks seventh all-time.

Iverson was inducted into the Naismith Memorial Basketball Hall of Fame in 2016.

2001-2002 Season

During the 2001-2002 season, Iverson continued his scoring dominance, averaging 31.4 points per game, which led the NBA. He also averaged 4.5 rebounds, 5.5 assists, and 2.8 steals per game.

Despite missing 22 games due to injuries, Iverson was selected to the All-NBA First Team and was named an NBA All-Star for the third consecutive season.

The 76ers finished the season with a 43-39 record and were eliminated in the first round of the playoffs by the Boston Celtics.
